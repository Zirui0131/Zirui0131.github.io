Abstract

This article is dedicated to a summarized description of existing test and reliability improvement methods developed so far for various MRAM technologies. The last part of this article gives some perspectives on this hot topic.

Introduction

MRAMs are as prone to defectivity and reliability issues as any other kind of memory.

Defectiveness and Reliability Issues of MRAMS

  • manufacturing process (defectiveness issues)
  • the lifetime of the memory (Note that these reliability issues may be the consequence of wear-out or aging mechanisms or inherent MTJ device parameter instability but can also be provoked by parameter deviations coming from an improper but acceptable manufacturing process and that will evolve over time.)

Defectiveness Issues

Strong or Weak Defects

  • fabrication steps
  • caused by the deposition of dust particles, voids occurring during the electrode polishing, oxide barrier breakdown, or improper etching followed by sideways material redeposition.
  • strong or weak depending on their influence on the system.
    • strong: prevent the device to operate correctly.
    • weak: prevent the device to operate correctly depending on the size of the defect.

Parametric Failures

  • Manufacturing process variation
    • magnetic process for the MTJs and a CMOS process for the access transistors and peripheries
    • variations come from changes in both material and lithographic properties, transistor electrical properties, and noise generated by thermal effects.

Reliability Issues

Impact of Process Variations

  • the resistance values can be deviated up to 4% from its nominal values
  • Due to the influence of process variation, the MTJ properties, such as resistance, TMR ratio, and switching delay, can be affected.
    • device parameter deviations due to errors of lithography or etching process
    • thermal disturbance
    • dielectric breakdown

Stochastic Switching

  • The switching of an MTJ is stochastic in nature, and the switching time is randomly distributed
  • some memory cells may not complete state transitions in a given write period
