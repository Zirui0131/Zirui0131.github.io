Electrical Modeling of STT-MRAM Defects

2018 IEEE International Test Conference (ITC)
INSPEC Accession Number: 18412682
DOI: 10.1109/TEST.2018.8624749

Abstract

Conventional STT-MRAM defect modeling, which is based on linear resistors, is too pessimistic in representing the real nature of physical defects. It may result in incorrect fault models, which in turn can lead to low-quality test solutions.

We propose a generic defect modeling methodology which captures the nonlinear behavior of STT-MRAM defects accurately; a defect is modeled by adjusting the affected STT-MRAM technology parameters.
- pinhole defect - STT-MRAM suffers from a fast transition between magnetic tunnel junction (MTJ) states with increased write current, making the MTJ more vulnerable to breakdown. - sidewall redeposition defect - a sidewall redeposition defect causes a fast transition without current elevation, which is not observed when using the conventional approach.

Smart Hammering: A practical method of pinhole detection in MRAM memories

2023 Design, Automation & Test in Europe Conference & Exhibition (DATE)
DOI: 10.23919/DATE56975.2023.10137267

Abstract

In this paper, we have statistically studied the behavior of pinholes and proposed a cost-effective testing scheme to capture pinhole defects and increase the reliability of the end product.

Introduction

  • Magnetic Tunnel Junction (MTJ) is manufactured during the back-end-of-line manufacturing phase. During this process, pinholes can be formed in the MgO layer of MTJs due to the diffusion of conducting materials (e.g., CoFeB) into the oxide layer.
  • These pinholes can grow in size during their life cycle and cause early-life failure in the memory.
  • This paper proposes a Memory Built-in Self- Test (MBIST) compatible, low-cost testing scheme for pinhole detection. It provides a guideline considering multiple factors, including process controllability, ECC budget, and defect coverage. The contributions of this paper are as follows.

#### three different categories - the cells with a relative pinhole area below 0.0015 - no data showing the failure of these cells within their expected lifetime - a relative pinhole area between 0.0015 and 0.0062 - the pinhole defect will cause a TMR degradation, the behavior of devices is still in the range of normal operation at the manufacturing test time - the pinhole defect area is larger than 0.0062 - the cell will fail during the standard March test

The pinholes in the MgO layer form a leakage path between two ferromagnetic layers and reduce the TMR value.
In some cases, the presence of pinholes could cause a field breakdown in the MgO layer due to the joule heating generated by the leakage current passing through the MTJ.

INSPEC Accession Number: 23123454
DOI: 10.1109/IRPS48203.2023.10118152

Abstract

  • a scale parameter dominating the endurance properties can be well predicted from breakdown voltage (BV) by taking into account a field acceleration parameter
  • the field acceleration parameter can be determined by dependence of BV on a voltage sweep rate

Introduction

  • It is necessary to establish an evaluation scheme for the accelerated degradation to establish the endurance property for such a highly reliable STT -MRAM
  • one needs to establish measurement scheme using the single MTJ device enabling prediction of highly reliable MRAM endurance properties more than 1010 cycles
  • In this study, instead of TDDB, we have comprehensively studied methodology how the endurance properties can be predicted from breakdown voltage (BV) measurement, as the required number of MTJs for BV is smaller than one for TDDB and the measurement time can be shorten compared with TDDB.
